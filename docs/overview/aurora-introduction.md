# Aurora Introduction

Aurora is a distributed guardian network for resilience, protection and network stability.

Its purpose is to help protect digital systems against large-scale attacks,
especially distributed denial-of-service (DDoS), overload conditions and coordinated network abuse.

Unlike traditional defensive systems that focus on defending a single target,
Aurora is based on a collective protection model:

**Attacks are not only blocked — they are distributed.**

By distributing load across trusted nodes, Aurora reduces the destructive impact
that concentrated attack traffic can have on a single service or location.

## Core Idea

Aurora follows a simple principle:

**No system stands alone.**

A protected service becomes part of a larger defense organism.
Traffic, telemetry, policy and trust signals are coordinated across the network
to improve resilience and reduce single points of failure.

## Role within the Aurora Ecosystem

Aurora is intended as the network-defense component of a larger protective architecture.

Its primary concerns are:

- network resilience
- attack absorption
- routing and failover
- telemetry-driven decisions
- node trust and participation control

## High-Level Capabilities

Aurora is being developed with the following capabilities in mind:

- trusted node registration
- telemetry ingestion
- policy distribution
- incident handling
- adaptive routing
- mitigation coordination
- PKI-based node identity
- distributed failover support
